About Us

Grounded in Community.
Guided by Care.

Black Moms NB exists because Black and African-descended families in New Brunswick deserve spaces that centre their experiences, voices, and needs.

Who We Are

A Community-Rooted Organization

Black Moms NB is a community-rooted organization supporting Black and African-descended children, youth, families, and communities through connection, advocacy, learning, and collective care.

We respond to what families and communities are navigating in real time — whether that’s systems navigation, finding resources, building connections, or simply having a space to share and be heard.

Our work is shaped by lived experience. The people behind Black Moms NB are community members themselves — which means we understand the barriers, know the gaps, and are committed to showing up in ways that actually matter.

“We create spaces for people to share experiences, access support, strengthen community connections, and navigate systemic barriers with guidance and care.”

Black Moms NB — New Brunswick, Canada

Our Focus Areas

  • Community Conversations Spaces for shared experience, reflection, and connection
  • Systems Navigation Walking alongside families through complex institutions
  • Resources & Tools Practical, accessible supports built from lived knowledge
  • Training & Collaboration Working with organizations to build better responses
What Guides Us

Our Values & Commitments

These values shape how we show up, how we work, and what we refuse to compromise on.

◉

Community First

Everything we do is shaped by and accountable to the community we serve. We don’t make decisions for community — we make them with community.

➤

Lived Experience as Knowledge

We centre the expertise that comes from living as a Black or African-descended person in New Brunswick. That knowledge is real, it is valid, and it guides our work.

▣

Accountability & Care

We hold ourselves to a standard of care — for the people we work with, for each other, and for the broader community whose trust we carry.

Why We Exist

Responding to What’s Real

Black and African-descended families in New Brunswick face real and compounding barriers — in education, health, housing, employment, and beyond. Black Moms NB exists because those barriers are real, and because community deserves more than systems that weren’t built with them in mind.

We don’t wait for institutions to fix themselves. We build the support, advocacy, and connection that families need right now.

What We Work Toward

  • → Institutions that respond with care and accountability
  • → Communities that are connected, resourced, and resilient
